Glyphloop / privacy

Your work stays yours.

Imported media

Images, videos, text, presets, and animations you create in the Studio are processed in your browser. Glyphloop does not upload or store that creative material.

Product analytics

Cloudflare Web Analytics measures aggregate page traffic and real-user performance. Cloudflare states that this service does not collect or use visitors' personal data.

The hosted website and Studio also record a small set of product events so we can learn whether visitors reach the Studio, import media, and complete an export. A random browser identifier and a per-tab session identifier are used to count return use and funnel completion.

Product analytics do not include IP addresses, user-agent strings, filenames, imported media, entered text, expressions, custom preset names, email addresses, or exported files. Events are retained by Cloudflare Analytics Engine for three months. Global Privacy Control and Do Not Track are honored.

Release-note email

If you request the 1.0 launch note, Glyphloop stores your email address, signup time, and the version of that consent in Cloudflare Workers KV. This information is used only to send one email when Glyphloop reaches 1.0, is not included in product analytics, and will be deleted after that email has been sent.

Addresses collected by the earlier public-beta launch form remain limited to that one public-beta launch email and will be deleted after it is sent. They are not silently moved onto the 1.0 list or any newsletter.

Open-source and command-line use

Source checkouts, the CLI, and the MCP server do not send product analytics to Glyphloop.
